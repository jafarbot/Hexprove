## Background

CEO requested removal of all security audit mentions from HexProve marketing materials. Before implementing, need legal review on whether disclaimers are protective or if complete removal is better.

## Current Situation

We currently mention "security audits" and recommend competitors (CertiK, Hacken) in several places.

## Legal Questions

### 1. Service Disclaimers
**Question:** Which approach provides better legal protection?

**Option A (Current):** Explicitly say "We don't do security audits"
- Potentially protective: makes clear what we don't offer
- Risk: Could someone claim we did security work if we ever catch a security bug?

**Option B (Proposed):** Never mention security audits at all
- Potentially protective: No confusion about our services
- Risk: Could someone claim we should have caught security issues?

**Legal team please advise:** Is it better to explicitly disclaim or not mention at all?

### 2. Recommending Other Vendors
**Question:** Can we recommend CertiK/Hacken without their permission or creating liability?

Current content says:
> "For smart contract security audits, we recommend working with specialized auditors like CertiK or Hacken."

**Concerns:**
- Do we need permission to recommend them?
- If they do poor work, could we have any liability for the recommendation?
- Does this create any partnership obligations?

**Legal team please advise:** Can we make these recommendations safely?

### 3. Naming Competitors in Internal Docs
**Question:** Can we name Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin, CertiK, etc. in our internal competitive analysis?

We have internal competitor docs that list these companies and analyze their positioning.

**Legal team please advise:** Is this standard competitive intelligence or could there be issues?

## Marketing VP's Recommendation (Non-Legal)

From a pure marketing perspective: **Remove all mentions**

**Rationale:**
- Talk about what we DO, not what we DON'T do
- Avoids SEO contamination (people searching for security audits)
- Prevents comparison with Trail of Bits/OpenZeppelin
- Clearer positioning: functional QA for crypto products

**Proposed replacement for FAQ:**
```
### What's the difference between QA testing and smart contract auditing?
We test the functional product experience—wallet connections, transaction flows, 
UI/UX, gas estimation, and user-facing features. This is separate from smart 
contract code audits. Most crypto products need both: audited contracts AND 
thorough product testing.
```

Or better: **Don't include this FAQ at all** unless a prospect asks.
