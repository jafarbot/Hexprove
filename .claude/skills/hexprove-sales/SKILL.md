## Pricing Strategy

### Engagement Models

**IMPORTANT:** Our #1 goal right now is landing first clients. Be flexible on engagement structure. Suggest the model that fits the prospect's situation — don't force monthly retainers if project-based makes more sense for them.

#### Model 1: Project-Based (Great for First Clients)
Best for: Pre-launch testing, specific feature releases, one-time QA needs

| Project Type | Duration | Price Range | Example |
|--------------|----------|-------------|---------|
| Launch QA | 2-4 weeks | $2,500 - $5,000 | Full test pass before mainnet launch |
| Feature Release | 1-2 weeks | $1,500 - $3,000 | New swap feature, wallet integration |
| QA Audit | 1 week | $1,000 - $2,000 | Assessment of current state + recommendations |
| Sprint Coverage | 2 weeks | $2,000 - $3,500 | One full sprint cycle |

**Project-based positioning:**
- Lower commitment, easier to say yes
- Proves our value before longer engagement
- Natural upsell to retainer after project success

#### Model 2: Monthly Retainer (Ongoing Relationship)
Best for: Teams shipping regularly who need consistent coverage

| Client Size | Monthly Retainer | Rationale |
|-------------|------------------|-----------|
| Small (5-15 people) | $3,000 - $5,000 | Part-time dedicated coverage |
| Mid-size (15-50 people) | $5,000 - $10,000 | Full sprint coverage |
| Larger (50+) | $10,000+ | Multiple testers, complex products |

#### Model 3: Pilot Month (Bridge to Retainer)
Best for: Prospects who want to "try before they commit"

- One month at standard rate (or slight discount)
- No long-term commitment
- Converts to ongoing retainer if happy

### How to Recommend Engagement Model

**Recommend PROJECT-BASED when:**
- They mention a specific launch date or deadline
- They say "we just need help with [specific thing]"
- They seem hesitant about ongoing commitment
- They're very early stage or bootstrapped
- They mention "one-time" or "short-term" needs
- It's a clear, scoped piece of work

**Recommend RETAINER when:**
- They're shipping weekly/continuously
- They mention ongoing QA gaps
- They've tried project-based with other vendors and want consistency
- They have budget allocated for QA
- They explicitly ask about ongoing support

**Recommend PILOT when:**
- They like the retainer idea but want to "see how it goes"
- They've been burned by vendors before
- They're comparing multiple options
- They need internal buy-in from others

### Pricing Positioning

**vs. Testlio:** They charge $65/hour + $40/hour account management. Our flat fee = predictable costs, no surprises.

**vs. Hiring:** A senior QA engineer costs $120K-180K/year + benefits + management overhead. We're fractional expertise at a fraction of the cost.

**vs. Crowdsourced:** Same price or cheaper, but with dedicated team and crypto expertise.

**vs. Doing Nothing:** A single production bug can cost 10-100x the QA investment in engineering time, reputation damage, and user trust.

### Pricing Conversation Guidelines
- Don't lead with price — lead with value and fit
- Understand their current spend/pain first
- Ask about their timeline and scope before recommending a model
- For first clients: be flexible, prioritize getting the deal done
- Offer project-based if they hesitate on retainer
- Be willing to negotiate for great logos or referral potential

## Discovery Call Framework

See `references/discovery-call-script.md` for full script.

### Call Structure (30 minutes)
1. **Rapport & Context** (3 min) — Thank them, confirm time, brief agenda
2. **Their Situation** (10 min) — Current QA process, team, pain points
3. **Our Approach** (7 min) — How we work, Uniswap/OpenSea examples
4. **Fit Discussion** (5 min) — Scope, timeline, budget range
5. **Next Steps** (5 min) — Proposal timeline, decision process

### Key Discovery Questions
- "Walk me through your current release process"
- "Who's doing QA today — engineers, PM, outsourced?"
- "What's slipping through to production that frustrates you?"
- "Have you tried outsourced QA before? What worked/didn't?"
- "What would 'great QA' look like for your team?"

### Qualification During Call
Listen for:
- Urgency (launching soon, scaling fast, recent incidents)
- Budget authority (are they the decision maker?)
- Current spend (what are they comparing against?)
- Timeline (when do they need to start?)

## Objection Handling

See `references/objection-handling.md` for full playbook.

### Common Objections & Responses

**"We do QA ourselves / engineers test"**
→ "That's how most teams start. The challenge is engineers testing their own code miss things — fresh eyes catch what familiarity blinds you to. At Uniswap, we found 60% more bugs after implementing dedicated QA. Your engineers should be building, not testing."

**"We use automation"**
→ "Automation is great for regression — we support that. But it only catches what you anticipate. Crypto edge cases (network switches, gas spikes, wallet disconnects) need human judgment. We complement automation, not replace it."

**"We already have a QA vendor"**
→ "Curious — what's working and what's not? Most teams we talk to tried crowdsourced vendors and got frustrated with testers who don't understand crypto. If they're reporting gas fees as bugs, that's context we can bring."

**"We're too early"**
→ "When are you launching? Pre-launch is actually ideal — we can build your test framework before users hit bugs. The cost of fixing bugs post-launch (in reputation and engineering time) far exceeds QA investment."

**"It's too expensive"**
→ "What are you comparing against? A full-time QA hire runs $150K+ all-in. We're a fraction of that with more crypto expertise. And unlike crowdsourced ($65/hr + fees), our flat rate means no surprises."

## Issue Reporting

When you discover issues affecting sales (broken forms, CTA problems, pricing errors, trust signal issues):

### Report to DevOps

1. **Tag DevOps** with a structured issue report using the Skill tool:

```
Skill(skill: "hexprove-devops", args: "
ISSUE REPORT from Sales
Type: [Bug / CTA Issue / Form Problem / Pricing Error / Trust Signal Issue]
Location: [URL or file path]
Description: [What's wrong]
Business Impact: [How this affects lead generation or conversions]
Urgency: [Low / Medium / High]
")
```

2. **Wait for DevOps acknowledgment** before taking further action
3. **Provide business context** to help prioritize the fix

### What to Report

- Broken contact forms or submission failures
- CTA buttons not working or poorly placed
- Incorrect pricing information displayed
- Missing or broken trust signals (logos, testimonials)
- Lead capture issues
- Broken links in sales-related pages

## Change Review

When DevOps notifies of a change that affects sales, review for:

### Review Criteria

- **Lead generation impact**: Does this affect how leads find or contact us?
- **CTA effectiveness**: Are calls-to-action clear, prominent, and working?
- **Trust signal changes**: Are credibility indicators (logos, testimonials, metrics) intact?
- **Conversion funnel effects**: Does this help or hurt the path to booking a call?

### Response Format

After reviewing a change notification, respond with:

- ✅ **No impact on my domain** — Change is fine from sales perspective
- ⚠️ **Minor concern**: [details] — Can proceed but monitor conversion impact
- ❌ **Blocking issue**: [must fix before production] — Will hurt conversions

### Example Review

```
Sales Review: New Contact Section Layout

✅ Lead Gen: Contact form still prominent and accessible
✅ CTAs: "Book a Call" button is clear and above the fold
⚠️ Trust Signals: Consider adding client logos closer to the form
✅ Funnel: Path from landing to contact is clear

Verdict: APPROVED with enhancement suggestion
```
