## Proposal Customization Guide

### Personalization Checklist

Before sending, ensure you've customized:

- [ ] Company name and description accurate
- [ ] Contact name and title correct
- [ ] Pain points reflect what they actually said
- [ ] Goals match their stated priorities
- [ ] Scope aligned with their product type
- [ ] Pricing appropriate for their size/needs
- [ ] Platforms listed match what they build
- [ ] Tools mentioned match what they use

### Pricing Option Guidelines

**Starter ($3-4K/month):**
- Small team (5-15 people)
- One platform focus
- Weekly releases or less
- Just getting started with dedicated QA

**Growth ($5-7K/month):**
- Mid-size team (15-30 people)
- Multiple platforms
- Weekly or bi-weekly releases
- Want consistent coverage

**Scale ($8-10K+/month):**
- Larger team (30+)
- Complex product (multiple products/platforms)
- Daily or continuous deployment
- Need embedded QA presence

### When to Offer Discount

Consider flexibility for:
- Great logo value (tier-1 company)
- Long-term commitment (3+ month upfront)
- Referral potential (well-connected founder)
- Strategic relationship (VC portfolio company)

Don't discount for:
- Tire-kickers who won't commit
- Companies that devalue the work
- Scope that doesn't justify price

### Follow-Up After Sending

**Same day:** Confirm receipt via quick email or Slack

**Day 3:** If no response, follow up
```
"Hey [Name], wanted to make sure the proposal landed. Any questions I can answer?"
```

**Day 7:** Second follow-up with added value
```
"Following up on the proposal. Happy to hop on a quick call to walk through it if easier. Also, [relevant news/insight] — thought you might find it interesting."
```

**Day 14:** Breakup email
```
"Hey [Name], haven't heard back so I'll assume the timing isn't right. No worries — if things change, I'm around. Good luck with [Company]."
```
